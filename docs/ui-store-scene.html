🏪 Store Scene UI Design
Interactive mockup based on GDD docs 15 · 17 · 25 · 26 · 29 · 30
Click any display tile or customer avatar to see the tooltip system.
LP Meter — The Core Prestige Bar
💎 Luxury Points are always visible
The LP bar lives permanently below the HUD. It shows current tier name, exact LP score, and next tier with the customer type it unlocks. This keeps the LP grind meaningful — players always know how close they are to a new customer tier. The gradient fill (purple → blue → gold) feels premium and unique vs. standard XP bars. LP never decreases, so the bar only ever grows.

Display Tooltip (4 key stats)
📦 What the tooltip shows
Stock (units remaining) · Value (coins/item + Fresh multiplier) · Supply Mode (Farm vs Supplier) · Today Earned (running total for that display). Below: which customer types will buy this display, and the item count sold today with the Fresh bonus percentage. Primary CTA is always Restock or Switch to Farm Mode depending on state.
LP Bar — Customer Unlock Badges
🏷️ Active Customer Type Badges
Small emerald pills below the LP bar show every customer tier currently visiting. This teaches players which customers they have (and which they don't). Locked tiers are shown greyed out with their LP requirement. Tapping any badge opens the Customers panel filtered to that type.
Customer Avatars
🚶 Real-time customer movement
Customers animate continuously across the store grid. A speech bubble shows their current action: Browsing... when looking, +30 💰 when purchasing (gold bubble), or ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Satisfied when leaving. Tapping a customer avatar shows their type, budget range, preference, and satisfaction history. Customers walk around blocked paths — dead-end aisles trap them.
Revenue Ticker
💰 Persistent earnings at a glance
A thin bar below the LP section shows today's total revenue and the current coins-per-minute rate. This rate updates live as customers buy items. A coin pop floats up from the display tile whenever a sale happens. The ⚡ Flash Sale 18m campaign badge shows active campaigns with a countdown.

Stockroom Panel
📦 Drag-to-restock UX
The stockroom panel shows items delivered by the Supply Lane runner. Each item card has a Restock → button. Tapping it highlights compatible empty/low displays on the grid. Tapping the highlighted display completes the restock. A one-tap Restock All button fills every display from available inventory automatically.
Campaign System
⚡ Campaign banner = action trigger
The campaign badge in the revenue ticker acts as a persistent reminder of the active campaign timer. Tapping opens the Campaign panel showing: active campaign progress bar + time left, and queued campaigns ready to launch. Campaigns are launched with Shopper Cards (earned via daily quests). Examples: Flash Sale (+50% budgets), Fresh Frenzy (Fresh displays sell 3× faster), Double LP (all LP gains doubled for 1 hour).
Aisle & Grid Layout Logic
🚶 Aisle width matters
The store grid shows a dedicated 1-tile-wide aisle row (grey) separating display sections. Customers enter from the bottom-left entrance tiles and exit at the right. A 2-tile-wide aisle is the recommended minimum — it allows 2 customers simultaneously with no throughput penalty. The entrance/exit indicators (↑↑/↓↓) show actual pathfinding. Players who block entrances see fewer customers visiting deeper displays.
